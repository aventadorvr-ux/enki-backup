**Review Cycle:** Annual  

---

## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

### 1.1 Purpose
This Information Security Policy establishes the framework for protecting Enki Real Estate's information assets, ensuring confidentiality, integrity, and availability in alignment with SOC 2 Type II Trust Services Criteria.

### 1.2 Scope
This policy applies to:
- All employees, contractors, and third-party vendors
- All information systems, networks, and data
- All facilities housing company assets
- Cloud services and third-party integrations

---

## 2. SECURITY PRINCIPLES

### 2.1 Confidentiality
Information shall be accessible only to those authorized to have access.

### 2.2 Integrity
Information shall be accurate and complete, protected from unauthorized modification.

### 2.3 Availability
Information and systems shall be accessible when required by authorized users.

### 2.4 Privacy
Personal information shall be collected, used, retained, and disclosed in accordance with applicable privacy laws (NZ Privacy Act 2020, GDPR where applicable).

---

## 3. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| Board of Directors | Ultimate accountability for security posture |
| CISO | Policy development, security program oversight |
| IT Security Team | Implementation, monitoring, incident response |
| Department Heads | Departmental security compliance |
| All Employees | Adherence to security policies and procedures |
| Third Parties | Contractual security obligations |

---

## 4. RISK MANAGEMENT

### 4.1 Risk Assessment
- Annual formal risk assessments
- Quarterly vulnerability assessments
- Continuous threat monitoring
- Risk treatment plans for identified risks

### 4.2 Risk Acceptance Criteria
- High risks: Board approval required
- Medium risks: CISO approval required
- Low risks: Department head approval

---

## 5. SECURITY CONTROLS FRAMEWORK

### 5.1 Administrative Controls
- Security awareness training (annual mandatory)
- Background verification procedures
- Acceptable use policies
- Security incident reporting procedures

### 5.2 Technical Controls
- Multi-factor authentication (MFA)
- Encryption at rest and in transit (AES-256, TLS 1.3)
- Intrusion detection/prevention systems
- Endpoint protection and EDR
- Security logging and monitoring
- Vulnerability management
- Patch management (critical: 7 days, high: 30 days)

### 5.3 Physical Controls
- Access-controlled facilities
- Visitor management procedures
- Environmental controls
- Equipment disposal procedures

---

## 6. ASSET MANAGEMENT

### 6.1 Asset Classification
| Classification | Description | Handling Requirements |
|----------------|-------------|----------------------|
| Critical | Business-critical data | Encryption, strict access controls, full audit trail |
| Confidential | Sensitive business/customer data | Encryption, need-to-know access |
| Internal | Internal use only | Standard access controls |
| Public | Approved for public release | No special handling |

### 6.2 Asset Inventory
- Maintained in centralized CMDB
- Quarterly verification
- Automated discovery for cloud assets

---

## 7. COMPLIANCE AND GOVERNANCE

### 7.1 Regulatory Compliance
- NZ Privacy Act 2020
- Real Estate Agents Act 2008
- GDPR (for EU data subjects)
- AML/CFT Act 2009

### 7.2 Audit Requirements
- Annual SOC 2 Type II audit
- Quarterly internal security assessments
- Continuous compliance monitoring

### 7.3 Policy Violations
- First violation: Warning and retraining
- Second violation: Formal disciplinary action
- Third violation: Termination (employment or contract)

---

## 8. POLICY REVIEW

This policy shall be reviewed:
- Annually at minimum
- Following any significant security incident
- Upon major organizational or technological changes
- When regulatory requirements change
