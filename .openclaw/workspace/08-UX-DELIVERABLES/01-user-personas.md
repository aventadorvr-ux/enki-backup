## Persona 1: Sarah Chen - The Established Agent

### Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name** | Sarah Chen |
| **Age** | 38 |
| **Role** | Senior Real Estate Agent |
| **Experience** | 12 years in real estate |
| **Market** | Auckland metropolitan |
| **Tech Comfort** | High |

### Background
Sarah is a top-performing agent at a boutique agency in Auckland's eastern suburbs. She specialises in family homes and investment properties. She's constantly juggling 15-20 active listings, numerous buyer inquiries, and vendor relationships. She values efficiency but refuses to compromise on the personal touch that built her reputation.

### Goals
- Close 3+ deals per month consistently
- Reduce time spent on repetitive tasks (data entry, scheduling)
- Provide vendors with data-driven pricing strategies
- Build long-term relationships with investors
- Maintain work-life balance (has two young children)

### Pain Points
| Pain Point | Frequency | Impact |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Qualifying leads takes 2-3 hours daily | Daily | High |
| Vendor expectations from unrealistic online valuations | Weekly | High |
| Missing out on hot listings due to delayed CMA preparation | Monthly | Critical |
| Manual follow-up tracking in spreadsheets | Daily | Medium |
| Explaining market conditions repeatedly | Weekly | Medium |

### Usage Patterns
- **Peak usage:** 7-9 AM (planning), 12-2 PM (admin), 6-8 PM (follow-ups)
- **Devices:** Desktop (morning office work), iPad (on-site), Phone (after hours)
- **Features used:** Lead pipeline, CMA generation, AI insights, mobile app

### Key Quote
> "I don't want to work harder—I want to work smarter. Show me which leads are actually ready to buy so I can focus there."

### Must-Have Features
- [ ] Lead qualification scoring
- [ ] One-click CMA generation
- [ ] Mobile listing updates from the field
- [ ] Automated follow-up reminders
- [ ] Price trend visualisations for vendor presentations

## Persona 3: Emma Taylor - The First-Time Investor

### Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name** | Emma Taylor |
| **Age** | 29 |
| **Role** | IT Professional / Aspiring Property Investor |
| **Experience** | First property purchase |
| **Income** | $95K (household $165K) |
| **Location** | Christchurch |

### Background
Emma is a software developer at a tech company in Christchurch. She's done her homework on property investment but is overwhelmed by conflicting information online. She's looking for her first investment property and values data-driven insights. She's comfortable with technology but expects everything to be mobile-optimized and intuitive.

### Goals
- Understand which suburbs offer best growth potential
- Identify properties with positive cashflow potential
- Learn the market without hours of research
- Build confidence before making major financial decisions
- Connect with trustworthy local agents

### Pain Points
| Pain Point | Frequency | Impact |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Overwhelming amount of conflicting online information | Daily | High |
| Can't interpret market statistics meaningfully | Monthly | Medium |
| Fear of overpaying without data backing | Weekly | High |
| Uncertain about the buying process timeline | Weekly | Medium |
| Difficulty comparing different properties apples-to-apples | Weekly | Medium |

### Usage Patterns
- **Peak usage:** 7-9 PM (after work), weekends
- **Devices:** Mobile phone (90%), occasional iPad
- **Features used:** AI assistant, suburb analysis, property comparisons, email alerts

### Key Quote
> "Don't give me a wall of statistics. Tell me what matters and why, in plain English."

### Must-Have Features
- [ ] Natural language AI assistant
- [ ] Suburb comparison tool with clear pros/cons
- [ ] Saved search alerts for new listings
- [ ] First-time buyer education content
- [ ] Easy mortgage/running cost calculators

## Persona 4: James O'Connor - The High-Volume Vendor

### Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Name** | James O'Connor |
| **Age** | 64 |
| **Role** | Retired / Property Portfolio Holder |
| **Experience** | 40+ years of property ownership |
| **Portfolio** | 12 properties (retiring/progressively selling) |
| **Location** | Currently in Auckland, considering downsizing to Bay of Plenty |

### Background
James built a successful property portfolio over 40 years and is now transitioning into retirement. He wants to sell 6-8 properties over the next 3 years to free up capital. He distrusts over-technology but values accuracy and his time. He prefers working with one trusted agent and expects white-glove service.

### Goals
- Achieve fair market value for properties
- Time sales with market cycles
- Minimise time spent on property management
- Understand CGT implications
- Find replacement coastal property to buy

### Pain Points
| Pain Point | Frequency | Impact |
|------------|-----------|--------|
| Overly optimistic appraisals from competing agents | Monthly | Critical |
| Keeping track of different properties' paperwork | Ongoing | Medium |
| Understanding current market vs. historical booms | Quarterly | High |
| Complex logistics when managing sales from remote locations | Quarterly | Medium |

### Usage Patterns
- **Peak usage:** 9-11 AM (phone calls with agent), occasional evening reviews
- **Devices:** iPad (reluctant user), Phone (calls primarily)
- **Features used:** Valuation reports, agent communication, simple mobile view

### Key Quote
> "I've been through four boom-bust cycles. Show me actual data, not predictions, and certainly not AI nonsense I can't understand."

### Must-Have Features
- [ ] Clear, printable valuation reports
- [ ] Low-friction communication with agent
- [ ] Price history visualisations (10+ years)
- [ ] Simple, jargon-free explanations
- [ ] Ability to delegate to adult children with dashboard access

## Cross-Persona Insights

### Universal Needs
1. **Trust signals** — proven accuracy, transparency about data sources
2. **Mobility** — tasks must be completable on mobile
3. **Time-saving** — automation of repetitive tasks
4. **Clarity** — information hierarchy that surfaces what matters

### Persona Conflicts
| Conflict | Resolution Strategy |
|----------|---------------------|
| Sarah wants AI insights; James distrusts "AI nonsense" | Show AI confidence levels; label clearly as "estimate" |
| Emma wants rich mobile features; James needs simplicity | Progressive disclosure; simple default, advanced optional |
| Marcus needs bulk actions; Sarah needs individual attention | Role-based UIs with appropriate views |
